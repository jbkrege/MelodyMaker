Welcome to Melody Maker!

The future of music making is here! Melody Maker is a suite of melody writting tools for want-to-be composers of all ages and skill levels. Develop your musical ideas in collaboration with AI trained with music written by the masters (well currently just folk music, but more to come!).

The interface is modeled after midi piano scrolls often found in DAWs (Digital Audio Workstations). It is diatonic, and in C major, with middle C (C4) in the center of your screen.
Press the computer button
to activate the AI composer. It will expand upon whatever existing melody you have written, providing insight based on its dataset and inspiration. To view available AIs, change the dataset, click on the settings button
. For a more random or complex result, turn up the temperature dial in the bottom tool bar.
The Directional Harmonisation button
simply displaces every note you've written by two spaces, but can be a good source of inspiration.
Open up the settings
panel to change other settings, like the length of the sequence, or to view this information again.

This project was created by Ben Krege, who dreamed of a tool like this in early 2016, but didn't have the time, expertise, or tools to make it until Fall 2018. Special thanks to Northwestern for funding the project through an undergraduate research grant (URG), and Nick Cline for helping Ben get the grant. Credits to Google Magenta/Tensorlflow teams, who created the neural net code that this application relies upon, and the Chrome Experiments Team, who's "Melody Maker" app was the basis for the interface.
